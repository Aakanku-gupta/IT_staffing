DIRECT HIRE & PERMANENT PLACEMENT

Permanent placement, on the other hand, may refer to the broader process of placing candidates into permanent positions, which may include both direct hires and placements made through staffing agencies or recruiters. In this case, the staffing agency or recruiter takes responsibility for sourcing and screening candidates, and may also provide additional services such as background checks, drug testing, or skills assessments. Once a suitable candidate is identified, they are placed into a permanent position with the client company. Both direct hire and permanent placement are commonly used in industries where there is a high demand for skilled workers, such as healthcare, IT, finance, and engineering. The key difference between the two is that direct hire involves the employer taking on all aspects of the hiring process, while permanent placement may involve the use of a third-party staffing agency or recruiter to assist in the process.
